### 4. How to Set Up Instant Match

Instant Match lets customers book you without sending a quote request first. This is where most paid Thumbtack leads come from.

1. Go to **Profile > Preferences > Instant Match**
2. Toggle ON for: TV Mounting, Furniture Assembly, Picture Hanging (these convert fastest)
3. Set your **travel fee**: $0 (absorb it — it wins more jobs vs. competitors charging $25+)
4. Set **job size preferences**: Min $100 job value
5. Set **availability**: Mon-Sat, 8am-8pm (match your actual hours exactly)
6. Set **response time target**: Under 1 hour (you already do this — make it official)
7. Enable **"Auto-respond"** with a custom message:

```
Hi! This is Handy & Friend. I received your request and will send a detailed quote within 1 hour. You can also reach me directly at (213) 361-1700. — Alex
```

### 5. Which Services to Bid On First (Highest ROI)

Priority order based on margin, ticket size, and Thumbtack competition level in LA:

**Tier 1 — Bid on immediately (high ticket, lower competition):**
1. **Cabinet Painting** — $95-$155/door, multi-door jobs = $500-$2,000+. Competitors underquote and underdeliver. Your upfront pricing wins.
2. **TV Mounting** — Fast job, $165-$250, high volume in LA. Repeat customers often need furniture assembly next.
3. **Interior Painting** — Large-ticket jobs. Bid on rooms 200 sq ft+. Cross-sell to flooring.

**Tier 2 — Bid on when you have bandwidth:**
4. **Furniture Assembly** — High volume (IKEA especially), quick jobs, strong cross-sell to TV mounting and art hanging.
5. **Flooring** — Higher effort to quote accurately but strong margins.

**Tier 3 — Accept but don't chase:**
6. Art/mirror hanging — Small ticket but quick. Only accept if bundled with TV mount or assembly.
7. Plumbing/Electrical — Good rate but quote-heavy. Accept inbound, don't actively bid.

**Skip or decline:**
- Landscaping, cleaning, moving — wrong category, wastes credits.

- Complete your profile 100% before spending a single dollar
- Use the free "Opportunities" tab to respond to open requests
- Thumbtack gives new pros free credits initially — use them for cabinet painting and TV mounting leads only

**Phase 2 — Start paid ($50-$75/week, Week 3+):**
- Only go paid after you have: 3+ reviews, complete profile, 2+ portfolio photos per service
- Start with **TV Mounting** and **Cabinet Painting** — these have the best cost-per-lead vs. ticket size in LA
- Set a **weekly budget cap** of $75 to start. Do not let it auto-scale.
- Target cost per lead: $15-$35. If you're paying $50+ per lead, pause and rewrite the service description.

**Phase 3 — Scale ($100-$150/week, Month 2+):**
- Only increase budget after you have 5+ reviews and are converting at least 1 in 4 leads to a booked job
- Add Interior Painting to paid bidding (higher lead cost but $800-$3,000 jobs)
- Never run paid Thumbtack for Plumbing/Electrical — lead cost is too high for minor work

**Rule of thumb:** If you spend $100 and get 1 booked TV mount ($165), that's a loss. You need 1 in 3 paid leads to convert. Track this weekly.

### 5. How to Get First 5 Reviews

**Do not ask randomly — follow this sequence:**

**Review #1-2: Past customers you already know**
Text or call 2 customers from recent jobs:
```
Hey [Name], it was great working with you on [job]. I just set up my Yelp page and would really appreciate a quick review — it helps a lot as a small business. Takes 2 minutes: yelp.com/biz/handy-and-friend-los-angeles. No pressure either way!
```

**Review #3: Current job, ask in person**
After finishing a job the customer is clearly happy with, say:
```
Really glad you're happy with it. If you have 2 minutes, a Yelp review would mean a lot to me — I'll text you the link right now.
```
Then text them immediately while you're still there. Conversion rate is much higher when asked in the moment.

**Review #4-5: Facebook or Nextdoor connections**
Post in your business Facebook page:
```
Just launched our Yelp page! If you've worked with Handy & Friend, we'd love a review. Every review helps us reach more homeowners in LA. Link in bio.
```

**What to say to customers to get a USEFUL review (not just 5 stars):**
Tell them: "Mentioning what we did and the neighborhood helps other people find us — even one sentence works."

**Never do:**
- Do not offer discounts for reviews (Yelp bans this)
- Do not ask customers to write reviews on your wifi (Yelp filters these as suspicious)
- Do not create fake reviews with friend accounts

### 6. Yelp Ads — When Worth It, When Not

**When Yelp Ads are NOT worth it:**
- You have fewer than 5 reviews — you will pay for clicks that don't convert because there is no social proof
- You have fewer than 10 photos — profile looks incomplete, money wasted
- You have not yet set up your services section with prices — leads will ask for quotes that could have been answered on the page
- You are below a 4.0 star average — ads will accelerate bad outcomes

**When Yelp Ads start to make sense:**
- You have 5+ reviews averaging 4.5+ stars
- Your profile is 100% complete (bio, services, photos, hours, website)
- You have been responding to all Yelp inquiries within 1 hour for at least 2 weeks
- You can track that at least 1 Yelp lead has converted to a booked job from your free listing

**Starting budget if you do run ads:**
- Start at $150/month maximum. Do not let Yelp auto-scale.
- Run ads for TV Mounting and Cabinet Painting only — highest ticket relative to effort
- Monitor cost-per-click weekly. In LA, expect $3-$8 per click. If above $10, pause.
- Run for 30 days, then calculate: total spend / jobs booked = cost per acquisition. If under $60, continue. If over $100, pause and improve profile first.

**The honest take:**
Most solo handymen in LA get better ROI from Thumbtack paid leads than Yelp Ads, at least in the first 6 months. Build Yelp reviews aggressively (they are free and permanent), but hold off on Yelp Ads until your Thumbtack conversion is proven.
