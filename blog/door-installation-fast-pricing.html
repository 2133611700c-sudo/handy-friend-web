Door Installation: Fast Pricing Without Surprises

Door installation quotes in Los Angeles move faster when slab, prehung, and exterior scopes are priced separately.

Slab vs prehung: cost and labor difference

A slab swap is generally faster when the existing frame is straight and hardware alignment is simple. Prehung doors include frame adjustments and usually require more prep. Exterior doors can add weather sealing and threshold alignment work. Listing door type first keeps quote accuracy high. Ask for line items instead of one blended number. Los Angeles scheduling also depends on corridor congestion between neighborhoods, so include your preferred arrival window and building access details in the first message. If your property has loading zone limits, include that note early to avoid rescheduling. A clear scope and access note usually shortens both dispatch time and on-site setup.

Hardware and swing direction checks

Before booking, confirm left-hand or right-hand swing and whether existing hardware will be reused. Misaligned latch and strike plates are common on older frames and should be considered in scope. If smart lock hardware is planned, include model details in advance. Small hardware notes can prevent schedule loss on install day. Always include a photo of hinges and jamb condition. For comparison, request a written quote with arrival window, included materials, and completion checklist in one message.

Paint, trim, and finishing expectations

Some installs are labor-only for fit, while others include trim touch-up and paint blending. Clarify if old casing removal or patching is expected. If finish painting is included, provide color and sheen before arrival. Keeping finish work separate makes both timeline and pricing easier to manage. Written handoff notes reduce callbacks. Include neighborhood and ZIP in your message so dispatch can estimate realistic same-day coverage.
