TV Mounting: What to Check Before You Book

A 60-second pre-check before booking TV mounting in Los Angeles can prevent return visits and extra wall work.

Wall type and anchor strategy

The same TV can require different hardware depending on drywall, masonry, or mixed backing. If you know stud spacing or wall composition, include it in the request. For rental units, check wall rules before drilling and keep owner approval in writing. If hidden wire routing is requested, include fireplace location and power outlet position. These details determine whether the standard or hidden-wire price tier is the right fit. Los Angeles scheduling also depends on corridor congestion between neighborhoods, so include your preferred arrival window and building access details in the first message. If your property has loading zone limits, include that note early to avoid rescheduling. A clear scope and access note usually shortens both dispatch time and on-site setup.

Viewing height and cable path planning

Most placement issues come from height choice rather than bracket quality. Share seating distance and eye-line preference so height can be set correctly on the first pass. Mention any console, soundbar, or wall feature that affects centering. Decide early whether cables will use cover channels or in-wall routing. A clear path plan avoids rework and keeps the finish clean. For comparison, request a written quote with arrival window, included materials, and completion checklist in one message.

What should be included in a written quote

A complete quote should include bracket install, leveling, cable plan, and cleanup. If patching is needed from prior holes, that should be listed separately. Ask whether hardware is included and whether additional material charges are possible. Confirm arrival window and expected duration. Written scope plus photos keeps same-day scheduling reliable. Include neighborhood and ZIP in your message so dispatch can estimate realistic same-day coverage.

Quick homeowner checklist

  1. Send one wide photo and one close-up of the exact work area.
  2. Add measurements, ZIP, and preferred time window.
  3. Confirm whether materials are supplied by homeowner or included in labor scope.
  4. Request a written total and note what can change the final amount.
  5. Approve only after arrival window and completion expectations are clear.
